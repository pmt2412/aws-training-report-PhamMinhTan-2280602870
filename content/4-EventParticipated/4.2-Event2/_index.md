# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day” (June 20)

### Event Objectives

- Create an interactive forum for interns and community members to exchange professional knowledge.
- Test and reinforce comprehensive knowledge through a highly competitive and strategic quiz format.
- Emphasize teamwork, case analysis, and risk management under time constraints.

### Format & Rules of Play

The event was highly dramatic, featuring **8 teams** competing in a **single-elimination tournament** format. Each round consisted of a head-to-head match between 2 teams: the team with the higher score advanced to the next round, while the losing team was eliminated.

**Rules for Answering Questions:**
- Each team selected a unique set of **10 multiple-choice questions**.
- The questions were divided into 3 point levels based on difficulty: **10 points, 20 points, and 50 points**.
- **Reward & Penalty Mechanism:** Correct answers awarded the question's point value, but incorrect answers deducted the exact same points from the team's score.

**Tactical Skills (Power-Ups):**
The most exciting aspect of the competition was that each team was given 2 special skills to reverse the situation or preserve their score, requiring careful strategic calculation:
1. **Star of Hope:** If activated and answered correctly, the team received **double points (x2)** for that question. However, the risk was that an incorrect answer would also result in a **double point deduction (x2)**.
2. **Risk Mitigation:** Provided a safe option. If answered correctly, the team only received **half points (1/2)**. In return, if answered incorrectly, **no points were deducted**.

### Key Takeaways & Personal Experience

Although I participated as an audience member watching the matches, I gathered many valuable lessons from observing the 8 competing teams:

- **Reviewing & Consolidating Knowledge:** Following the questions and answers served as a practical knowledge review. The questions ranging from 10 to 50 points helped me identify gaps in my own knowledge to continue cultivating.
- **Risk Management & Tactical Mindset:** The game was a miniature simulation of real-world project decision-making. By observing how teams deployed the "Star of Hope" to accelerate or stage a comeback, or used "Risk Mitigation" on difficult questions to protect their score, I learned about evaluating success probabilities and the art of risk management. Knowing when to take risks and when to play defensively is crucial.
- **The Importance of Teamwork:** The pressure of point deductions made every decision count. I observed that teams with good communication, clear role division, and trust in mutual decisions had a much higher success rate.
